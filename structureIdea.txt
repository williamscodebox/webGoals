A personal website for a web developer should be more than just a digital résumé—it should be a living showcase of your skills, personality, and professionalism. Here's a layout that balances clarity, creativity, and conversion:

🧱 Suggested Layout Structure
1. Hero Section (First Impression)
Your name + title: e.g., “Hi, I’m Alex — Full-Stack Web Developer”

Short tagline: What you specialize in or your mission

Call to Action: “View My Work” or “Hire Me”

Optional: subtle animation or interactive background

2. About Me
A short bio with a friendly tone

A photo or avatar

Highlights: years of experience, tech stack, industries you've worked in

Optional: downloadable résumé

3. Skills & Tech Stack
Icons or badges for languages, frameworks, tools

Grouped by category: Frontend, Backend, DevOps, etc.

Optional: progress bars or experience levels

4. Portfolio / Projects
Grid or carousel layout

Each project should include:

Title + short description

Tech used

Screenshot or demo link

GitHub or live site link

5. Testimonials (Social Proof)
Quotes from clients, collaborators, or employers

Include names, roles, and optionally photos

6. Blog or Insights (Optional but powerful)
Share tutorials, case studies, or dev thoughts

Helps with SEO and shows thought leadership

7. Contact Section
Simple form: name, email, message

Direct email link

Social media icons (LinkedIn, GitHub, Twitter)

8. Footer
Copyright

Navigation links

Optional: dark/light mode toggle

🎨 Design Tips
Keep it responsive: Mobile-first design is a must

Use whitespace: Clean layouts feel more professional

Typography matters: Choose readable fonts with personality

Color palette: Stick to 2–3 core colors that reflect your brand

🔥 Bonus Ideas
Add a “Now” page: What you're currently working on

Include a “Uses” page: Tools, gear, and software you use

Add subtle micro-interactions: Hover effects, scroll animations

Use a custom domain: e.g., yourname.dev or yourname.me
